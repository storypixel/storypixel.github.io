General info

  • Play calls name targets by number: the person on the far left is 1, next is 2, and so on.
  • On nearly every call, players who aren't throwing should be pump faking.
  • Corners are the players on the far-left and far-right of the court.
  • Listen for "ball left" / "ball right" — send a ball to that corner.

3-ball offensive calls

  • Huddle — Meet in the middle at the back line and decide which of the three ball-holders throws. (Usually Josh and Daniel play-call when they're on the court.)
  • If a corner throws, the middle player shifts slightly toward that side and back to protect the corner as they backpedal. Don't pass the corner the balls until they're at/near the back line, and wait until the other team isn't in an active/ready position. The ball-holder stays active like a corner would.
  • Almost always keep two balls.
  • Non-throwing players keep pump faking.

4-ball offensive throws

Options to call during the huddle:

  • Single-ball throw — Huddle, designate one thrower. Try to keep 3 balls for defense (not a hard rule if you see a good opening). Call it to burn clock or when we're down players.
  • Kill left / right — The two players on that side throw at the same target. "Kill left on 3" = the left two ball-holders aim at the 3rd player from the left on the other team.
  • Insides — The two middle players pick a target. "Insides on 5" = the middle two aim at the 5th player from the left.

5 or 6 ball calls

  • Same calls as 4-ball if we have 4+ players: huddle and discuss the target. Here we can put 3 throwers on a single target.

2–3 ball defensive calls

  • Each corner should have a ball if feasible.
  • Red light — Do not throw. Hold the ball but actively push the other team back and keep them off balance with pump fakes.
  • Green light — The designated corner may counter the other team's attack (doesn't have to throw without a good opening). Only one corner has green light at a time.
  • Home (usually a 3-ball call) — The ball-holder opposite the opposing thrower throws at them from the back line, or near where they were standing when the other player released.
  • Away — All ball-holders rush the center line; the one opposite the thrower throws at them from at/near the center line.
  • Crash (2-ball call) — If we're up players and they have only 1–2 left: after they throw, the two corners holding balls both rush and attack. If more than one is left, target the player who just threw.

1-ball defensive call

  • Middle — Pass to the middle player; they take an offensive position to bait throws so we regain ball control. Optional, for confident blockers or if you're feeling spicy. Can also go to a corner to take an offensive pump-fake position.

Miscellaneous calls

  • Speed ball — Throw quickly, don't sweat ball control. Good late in games when we're down, or just for fun.
  • Have fun — Do whatever the f*** you want, just try not to give up all the balls.
  • Pitch back — On the opening rush, a rusher quickly tosses a ball back to a designated player who can target anyone. Double pitch back = toss both balls to two players; one of them releases.

Rule sets — TPSL adjustments

  • Not 3-minute games rolling into no-block. Instead: a 22-minute game of regular play, then 3 minutes of no blocking.
  • About 10 players on the court.
  • We can shag, but only out the back line with a hand raised.
