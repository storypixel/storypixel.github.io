What's it mean

Futures are pulling back a touch this morning with the S&P off about 13 and the Nasdaq down 127, taking a little air out of the recent chip-stock frenzy that's been pushing indexes to new records. The big one on tap is CPI at 7:30. Over the last few years a hot CPI print has meant the Fed stays put on rates and that's been a clear negative for stocks, especially the high-multiple tech names that have been doing all the heavy lifting. This morning however oil is ripping over a buck on stalled Mideast diplomacy, which could quietly feed right back into that inflation number. Watch the 10-year yield at 4.43 — if CPI runs hot and yields push higher, those chip names that have led this rally are the ones most at risk of giving some back.
