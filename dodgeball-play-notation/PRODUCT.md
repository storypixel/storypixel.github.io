# Dodgeball Playbook Product Context

## Register

Product

## Users

Dodgeball coaches and players use the playbook to browse calls, understand sequences, and review animated positioning. They often check it quickly on a phone at practice or courtside.

## Product Purpose

Make each play easy to find, read, share, and animate without requiring users to understand the underlying notation.

## Brand Personality

Plain, direct, utilitarian, and deliberately old-web. The outer playbook is a basic black-and-white serif reference page. The court animator is a separate embedded tool with its own isolated visual system.

## Anti-references

- No polished SaaS shell, decorative cards, gradients, or ornamental motion.
- No bloated controls or options that distract from the play.
- No page CSS that leaks into the embedded animator.
- No tiny mobile type, desktop-only spacing, or off-center content.

## Design Principles

- Put the play and its sequence first.
- Preserve the simple 1995 HTML character of the outer page.
- Keep the animator's styles isolated and self-contained.
- Adapt layout, type, and controls to the available width.
- Prefer readable, familiar browser behavior over visual novelty.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

Body copy must remain readable at phone widths, controls must work with touch and keyboard, text must scale cleanly, and reduced-motion preferences must be respected. The page should not create horizontal scrolling at 320 CSS pixels or wider.
