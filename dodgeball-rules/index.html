Format

  • Balls — The game is played with 5 foam balls (3 small foam balls and 2 large ones).
  • Boundaries — The court is the size of a basketball court, or extended horizontally to fit larger groups. If extended for excess players, it may be shrunk at general discretion once a reasonable number from one or both sides are out. All lines are soft and can be stepped on. Players crossing the center line on a live throw may only cross a "reasonable" amount.
  • Scoring — Each team is awarded one point for each game where all players from the opposite side are eliminated.
  • Live Ball — A ball is live from the time it leaves a player's hand until it touches a dead object.
  • Dead Objects — An out player, the floor / wall / ceiling, or another ball in mid-flight.

Game Play

  • Rush — Each team starts with one small ball. The start of each game has players from both sides attempting to gain control of the three contested balls on the center line.
  • Burden — A player counts down from five and all balls in a player's possession must be relinquished by zero. If a player is holding two balls, they only have to relinquish one. If a player holding a ball is hit before the count ends, that ball doesn't need to be thrown.
  • Valid Hit — A player hit by a live ball on any part of their body or clothing is out. A player can be hit by a live ball when behind the back line, but cannot be hit while outside the sideline retrieving a ball.
  • Valid Catch — A player catches a live ball thrown by the opposing team with at least one point of contact in the court boundaries, and must show meaningful control for the catch to count. If a player catches a ball and is then struck by another ball, the catch is valid if they demonstrated control of the caught ball before the other ball died.
  • A player may catch without a point of contact in the court boundaries if the opposing team was attempting a basket and the player had at least one point of contact with the court when the ball was released.
  • On a successful catch, the first dead player from the catching team returns as a live player. A caught player is live once they establish one point of contact with the court.
  • Basket — Teams may shoot a basket when they have two or fewer live players on the court. If a basket is made, that whole team re-enters as live players. If the court was shrunk from the initial size, it doesn't expand back until the start of the next game.
  • Shagging — Dead players may shag balls from the sideline, including reaching onto the court to stop a ball actively in play. Live players may step off the court to shag but are still eligible to be hit behind the back line (not along the sidelines).
